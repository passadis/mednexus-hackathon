Patient Interview Transcript
Date: 2026-03-09
Interviewer: Dr. Sarah Jenkins

Patient states: "I was playing in my Sunday league soccer match yesterday, and another player and I went for a 50/50 ball. I ended up kicking the hard plastic sole of his cleat instead of the ball. My right big toe immediately started throbbing. By the time I took my boot off after the game, it was pretty swollen and starting to turn purple. It really hurts to put full weight on it when I walk, especially when pushing off."

Vital signs: BP 118/76, HR 68, Temp 98.4°F, SpO2 99%
Observation: Moderate edema (swelling) and ecchymosis (bruising) noted over the dorsal aspect of the right hallux (big toe), specifically around the distal phalanx. Capillary refill is < 2 seconds, indicating good blood flow. Significant point tenderness upon palpation. Range of motion in the toe is limited secondary to pain and swelling.

History: 28 y/o male, active recreational athlete. No significant past medical history. No prior fractures or orthopedic surgeries.
Current medications: Ibuprofen 400mg PRN (patient reports taking two doses since the injury).

Patient denies: Numbness, tingling, feeling a "pop" or "snap" at the time of injury, pain in the ankle or other toes, or previous injuries to this foot.

Assessment: Acute trauma to the right hallux sustained during athletic activity. Symptoms and physical exam are consistent with a severe contusion or sprain, but a non-displaced fracture of the phalanx cannot be definitively ruled out clinically. Ordering an X-ray of the right foot (AP, lateral, and oblique views) to check for bony injury. Patient advised to continue R.I.C.E. protocol (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) and use a stiff-soled shoe or walking boot pending imaging results.
